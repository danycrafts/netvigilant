# Project Governance

This document outlines the governance model for the project.

## Principles

*   **Openness**: All discussions, decisions, and work are conducted in the open.
*   **Fairness**: All contributors are treated equally and respectfully.
*   **Transparency**: Decisions are documented and accessible.
*   **Meritocracy**: Influence is earned through contributions to the project.

## Roles and Responsibilities

### Contributors

Anyone who contributes to the project, whether through code, documentation, bug reports, or other means.

### Maintainers

Maintainers are responsible for the overall health and direction of the project, including:
*   Reviewing and merging pull requests.
*   Triaging issues.
*   Making release decisions.
*   Mentoring new contributors.

### Project Lead (Optional)

A designated individual or small group responsible for strategic direction, conflict resolution, and ensuring the project aligns with its vision.

## Decision Making

Decisions are made by consensus among the maintainers. If consensus cannot be reached, the project lead (if applicable) will make the final decision.

## Becoming a Maintainer

New maintainers are proposed by existing maintainers and approved by the current maintainer team based on:
*   Sustained, high-quality contributions to the project.
*   Expertise in relevant areas.
*   Commitment to the project's vision and values.

## Code of Conduct

All participants in this project are expected to adhere to the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

## Amendments

This governance model may be amended by a supermajority vote of the maintainers.
